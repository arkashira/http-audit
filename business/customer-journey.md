## Customer Journey Map for HTTP-Audit

### 1. Aware
- **Trigger Event:** System administrators and security professionals recognize the need for enhanced monitoring of HTTP server interactions due to increasing security threats and compliance requirements.
- **Friction Points:** Lack of awareness about existing solutions, overwhelming amount of information on security tools, and difficulty in identifying a tool that integrates well with Linux.
- **User Emotions:** Frustration and confusion about the best tools to use for their specific needs.
- **Opportunities to Delight:** Provide educational content (blogs, webinars) on the importance of HTTP auditing and how HTTP-Audit can meet these needs. Offer free resources or guides to help users understand the product's benefits.
- **Metric per Phase:** Number of website visits to product landing page, engagement with educational content.

### 2. Consider
- **Trigger Event:** Users actively search for solutions to monitor HTTP server interactions and come across HTTP-Audit.
- **Friction Points:** Comparing features with competitors, uncertainty about the effectiveness of the tool, and concerns about integration with existing systems.
- **User Emotions:** Hopeful yet skeptical about finding a solution that fits their needs.
- **Opportunities to Delight:** Offer detailed comparison charts, customer testimonials, and case studies that highlight successful implementations. Provide a clear FAQ section addressing common concerns.
- **Metric per Phase:** Number of demo requests, downloads of product documentation.

### 3. Try
- **Trigger Event:** Users decide to test HTTP-Audit to evaluate its capabilities.
- **Friction Points:** Installation challenges, initial configuration complexity, and lack of immediate support.
- **User Emotions:** Excitement mixed with anxiety about the setup process and whether the tool will meet their expectations.
- **Opportunities to Delight:** Provide a guided installation wizard, comprehensive setup tutorials, and responsive customer support during the trial period. Consider offering a limited-time free trial to encourage usage.
- **Metric per Phase:** Number of installations, feedback from trial users.

### 4. Adopt
- **Trigger Event:** Users successfully implement HTTP-Audit and begin to see its benefits in monitoring HTTP interactions.
- **Friction Points:** Learning curve associated with advanced features, potential performance issues, and integration with other security tools.
- **User Emotions:** Satisfaction from successful implementation but may feel overwhelmed by advanced features.
- **Opportunities to Delight:** Offer personalized onboarding sessions, advanced feature webinars, and a dedicated customer success manager to assist with integration and usage.
- **Metric per Phase:** User retention rate, frequency of feature usage.

### 5. Expand
- **Trigger Event:** Users recognize additional needs for monitoring or security and consider expanding their use of HTTP-Audit or related products.
- **Friction Points:** Budget constraints, uncertainty about the ROI of expanding usage, and potential resistance from stakeholders.
- **User Emotions:** Ambivalence about spending more but excited about the potential benefits of expanded capabilities.
- **Opportunities to Delight:** Provide clear ROI calculations, bundle offers for additional features, and loyalty discounts for existing customers. Share success stories of other users who expanded their usage.
- **Metric per Phase:** Upsell conversion rate, customer lifetime value (CLV).
